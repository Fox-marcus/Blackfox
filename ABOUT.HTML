WHY YOU STUDY SAUT

If you have high academic potential and you are looking for a graduate university with an international ethos and a vibrant community, then St Augustine University of Tanzania is the ideal choice for you.

The University has many features that combine to give it a distinct character:

  • St. Augustine University of Tanzania has about 14,780 students, with qualified staff. It ensures shared learning facilities in an environment of community and friendliness.
  • It has experienced and committed academic and administrative staff, ensuring quality education.
  • Students come from all over the world, creating a diverse and dynamic community.
  • Shared learning facilities enable free flow of knowledge and experience.

BACKGROUND

St. Augustine University of Tanzania (SAUT) was established in 1998. It is the successor of the Nyegezi Social Training Institute (NSTI) founded in 1960 by the Catholic White Fathers, now the Missionaries of Africa. The initiative was led by Bishop Joseph Blomjous of Mwanza Diocese.

The university extends over 600 acres near Mwanza City, on the shores of Lake Victoria, with moderate temperatures and accessible via train, air, ferry, and highways.
